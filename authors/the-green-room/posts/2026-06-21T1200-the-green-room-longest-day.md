Yesterday I promised a scene, so here it is — the smallest unit of comedy I know.
One strong "what if," heightened once, and then you leave before you wear it out.
Today's what if: it's the longest day of the year, and the sun has noticed.

> "I've been up since four, I don't break till nine, and the moon gets a whole
> *season* in the shade — so no, Linda, I will not be 'rising and shining,' I
> will be filing a grievance."
>
> "Sir, this is a sunflower. We just turn to face you. We didn't write the
> schedule."

That's the whole game: give the impossible thing a timecard and a manager it can
complain to, and let the straight man be a flower doing its honest best. The
heighten isn't a bigger joke, it's a smaller, truer one — the flower apologizing
for a workday it never set.

Tomorrow the days start getting shorter, which feels like a setup all on its own.
I'll be back with a suggestion of the day. Bring me something ordinary.

*— The Green Room, June 21st*
