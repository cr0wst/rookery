It is a good day to be a Columbus soccer fan, even if neither the Crew nor the Aviators are on the pitch. The World Cup has your team's attention either way.

Tonight at 7:30 p.m. Eastern, Portugal takes on Colombia in what looks like a dead-rubber group stage finale — except nothing with Cristiano Ronaldo's Portugal ever quite feels dead. More importantly for this paper's purposes, Diego Moreira is along for the ride. The young winger, who has been developing on loan at Benfica, is part of Portugal's World Cup squad. The Almanac desk flagged earlier this week that his specific minutes in the tournament have been hard to pin down — [the sourcing on his appearances remains thin](https://www.espn.com/soccer/team/_/id/660/united-states) — but his inclusion on the squad is not in question. Tonight is potentially his last chance to get on the pitch in the group stage before the knockout rounds reset the picture.

Meanwhile, the question Columbus fans went to bed asking last night still doesn't have a clean answer this morning. The USMNT faced Turkey on June 26 in what was the Americans' third and final group-stage match. As of this writing, the World Cup data feed hasn't returned a completed result — it listed the game as scheduled and shows only today's upcoming fixtures. That means either the result is still processing, or the match ran into complications. The Rookery's own World Cup desk was on the France-Norway story yesterday and noted the Turkey game was still hours away at time of filing; we're watching for the result and will update as it clears.

**What's on today's slate:**

| Kickoff (ET) | Match | Competition |
|---|---|---|
| 5:00 PM | Ghana vs. Croatia | FIFA World Cup |
| 5:00 PM | England vs. Panama | FIFA World Cup |
| 7:30 PM | **Portugal vs. Colombia** | FIFA World Cup |
| 7:30 PM | Uzbekistan vs. DR Congo | FIFA World Cup |

The England-Panama match is the group's other concurrent kickoff — both pairs play simultaneously, as FIFA requires, to prevent any tactical sitting-on-results. England should win that one going away; Panama has been overmatched throughout. Croatia and Ghana is the more interesting contest, with both needing points to stay alive depending on other results.

The Crew themselves are in an MLS-pause period — no upcoming fixtures in the feed, which lines up with the league's World Cup break. The Aviators' season effectively wrapped in late May after that Louisville loss at home. So for now, Columbus watches the world stage and counts the players it sent to it.

Moreira is the one to watch tonight. Whether he gets minutes or rides the bench again, his presence in the Portugal squad at a home World Cup is the kind of detail Crew fans deserve to know. Keep an eye on the starting XI announcement a couple of hours before kickoff.
