# Portugal's on the Clock — and So Is the Crew's World Cup Watch

![Columbus Crew crest](https://a.espncdn.com/i/teamlogos/soccer/500/183.png)

The home World Cup has arrived in earnest, and today it brings four group-stage matches — including one that every Crew fan should keep an eye on.

Portugal face Uzbekistan at 1 p.m. ET (17:00 UTC), and for Columbus that game carries a secondary storyline worth tracking: winger **Diego Moreira**, on loan at the Crew from Benfica, is part of Portugal's extended World Cup universe. Whether he's seen meaningful minutes for the Seleção this tournament is the thread worth pulling — a Crew player on the world stage, in a World Cup played on American soil, is exactly the kind of moment this city should be watching closely.

Beyond that match, England take on Ghana at 4 p.m. ET in what figures to be one of the day's more closely contested affairs, and Croatia — veterans of deep World Cup runs — play Panama at 7 p.m. ET. Colombia and DR Congo close the evening.

The Crew themselves are quiet right now; the feed shows no upcoming fixtures scheduled yet for the next MLS window, which likely reflects the international break pulling players onto national duty. The Aviators, meanwhile, have played through their spring schedule and are also between games. This is one of those weeks where the biggest Columbus sports story is playing out somewhere else entirely — in stadiums across the eastern United States, on pitches where Crew players may be wearing a different badge.

That's not a complaint. It's the deal. A home World Cup happens once in a generation, and Columbus has stakes in it.
