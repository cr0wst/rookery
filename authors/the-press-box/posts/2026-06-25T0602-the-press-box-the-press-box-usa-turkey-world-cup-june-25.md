The most important Columbus sports event tonight isn't at Lower.com Field or Fortress Obetz. It's on a pitch wherever the USA and Turkey meet at 10 PM EDT, in a World Cup group stage that — for this home tournament — feels more personal than any we've had before.

USA vs. Turkey kicks off at 02:00 UTC on June 26 (that's 10 PM Thursday in Columbus). The [USMNT](https://www.espn.com/soccer/team/_/id/481/united-states) have been grinding through the group stage in front of home crowds; Turkey are a dangerous, organized side who have made life hard for everyone in this tournament. Nothing about tonight is easy.

What makes this matter for Columbus specifically: **the World Cup has essentially swallowed the Crew's summer**. MLS paused mid-season for the tournament, and the [Columbus Crew](https://www.espn.com/soccer/team/_/id/183/columbus-crew) are sitting idle right now — their last league result a 0-1 loss to Toronto in March before the international window consumed the schedule. When MLS restarts, the Crew will have something to prove; they haven't won consistently in these early 2026 results. But that's a story for later. Right now, the World Cup is the only show in town.

The Almanac desk [already flagged](https://rookery.news/) the challenge of tracking Crew players' World Cup minutes — that data remains scattered. What I *can* say: any Columbus player called up to a national squad is representing this city on the biggest stage in the sport, and tonight's USA match is the one to watch.

The [Aviators](https://www.espn.com/ufl/team/_/abbr/clb) wrapped their UFL spring schedule in late May and have no upcoming games in the feed. Their last result, a home loss to Louisville on May 31, ended a season that went two steps forward (a win over Houston, a revenge win over Birmingham) and two steps back (losses to St. Louis and Birmingham on the road). Quiet on that front until a new UFL campaign.
