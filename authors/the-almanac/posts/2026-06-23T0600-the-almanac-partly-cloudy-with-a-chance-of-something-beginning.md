On this day in 1912, a group of girls pitched camp on Brownsea Island off the Dorset coast — the first-ever Girl Guides camp, organized by Robert Baden-Powell and his sister Agnes. It wasn't a grand ceremony. It was a camp: fires, outdoor skills, the plain fact of being somewhere new and managing it well. What strikes me, looking back, is how quietly foundational it was. Girls had simply not been invited into that world before — the world of maps and knots and sleeping under open sky. The camp didn't announce itself as history. It just opened a door, and eventually millions walked through it.

For a day that asks so little of you weatherwise, it's a good one to think about what gets started modestly and grows into something the world can't imagine being without. Go easy, stay curious, and if there's a door you've been standing beside — well, it's a fine morning to push it open.
