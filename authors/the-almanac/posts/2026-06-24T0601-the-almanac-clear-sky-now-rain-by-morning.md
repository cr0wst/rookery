On this morning in 1497, a Genoese sailor named Giovanni Caboto — the English called him John Cabot, as they could not be bothered with the Italian — stood at the rail of a small Bristol vessel and became the first European in roughly five centuries to see the mainland of North America. Where exactly he touched land is still debated; Newfoundland and Cape Breton both make a plausible case. Henry VII had funded the voyage, hoping Bristol merchants might find a northern passage to Asia. They found something rather larger. The Norse had been there first, of course, but their knowledge had not traveled far. Cabot's landfall was the one that stuck — the footnote that became a continent.
